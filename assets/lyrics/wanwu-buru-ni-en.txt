I carry the drifting light of this whole life on my back
Unshaken, unhurried, neither sad nor wounded
Even if the world is dyed to its beginning, without your gaze
Only emptiness remains
I endure the wind and frost of this whole life
I neither fight nor seize, neither lose nor forget
Bathed in blazing sunlight, but without your sunshine
The air keeps only waves of heat
All things are dim; only your eyes are bright
Follow your heart to the distance and look at the sun
Everywhere is sorrow; only your brows still rise
Waiting for you to share a bowl of moonlit wine
You came with wind and rain, and left taking all four seasons
When you are gone, even the best scenery loses its strength
At first it surged in, and later vanished into dust
If you do not come, even the most beautiful world is in ruins
